# Working context: a model

The pages under `/theory` describe what working context *is*, prior to any system that handles it. The model is a semantic specification, not an engineering brief. It does not say what Temper does. It says what kind of thing working context is, which is what any system serving the work has to honor.

## The model exists in service of attention

The manifesto names attention as the most precious thing a perspective brings to its work — finite, costly, non-renewable in any meaningful sense. The model inherits that anchor.

Every primitive below — what makes a position, what makes a field, what makes a perspective, what makes a deformation — is chosen because it generates design constraints that respect attention rather than spending it. From the anchor follow: the system pre-pays the cost of common queries by codifying expected patterns. The system makes perspective-differences visible at translation points. The system surfaces traces of past error at corrected regions. The system never demands re-construction from scratch when reasonable defaults exist.

The anchor also produces negative constraints — things the model deliberately rules out. Anything that demands attention be spent re-discovering what the system could have made present. Anything that hides differences a perspective will eventually need to confront. Anything that privileges completeness over relevance. These are derivable from the anchor, not invented case-by-case.

## One commitment worth naming up front

The model's most consequential claim is about what is *stored*. The system stores data and traces of past intentional acts — recorded questions, notes, decisions, which themselves become further data. It does not store *knowledge*. Knowledge is the relationship between a perspective and the information that perspective produces through engagement with data.

The label "knowledge base" is a misnomer in light of this. Knowledge is always potential, never actual, until activated by a perspective. The system's job is not to be right about what something means — meaning is not in the system. Its job is to faithfully represent data, faithfully record intentions, and faithfully compute projections such that perspectives engaging with those projections are well-equipped to produce knowledge.

Everything downstream — the manifold, the fields, the deformations, the perspectives, the translation problem — sits inside this frame.

## The shape of the model

The pages here introduce the model in the order the source document does. Each is short; each can be read alone; the sequence is the most coherent path through.

- **[Ontology](/theory/ontology)** — Data, intention, information, knowledge. The stratified layers.
- **[Manifold](/theory/manifold)** — Positions, fields, streams. The geometry.
- **[Time](/theory/time)** — Time as a primary axis. Events-as-primary. Why this is a substrate commitment.
- **[Deformation](/theory/deformation)** — Forming and forgetting. Strong vs. weak. Scarification. Self-cohesion and relaxation.
- **[Perspectives](/theory/perspectives)** — Trajectories, not points. Role-perspective vs. individual. Access vs. expertise.
- **[Translation](/theory/translation)** — Why translation is irreducible. Bridges. Knowledge as relationship.

Two reference surfaces sit alongside:

- **[Schema](/theory/schema)** — The structural codification: entity types, event structure, topic taxonomy, resolved stances. Work in progress.
- **[Open questions](/theory/open-questions)** — What is not yet settled. Updates over time as items resolve.

The model is provisional. It captures a mental picture clearly enough to be argued with. Pages link out to the open-questions surface where a section's framing depends on an unresolved decision.

## Editorial notes

- Reader arrives from `/` and has already accepted attention as anchor. This page does not re-prosecute that argument; it re-states briefly and gets to the model's shape.
- The *knowledge-bases-are-misnomers* commitment is named on this page rather than buried in ontology because it is the model's most provocative claim and a reader who stops reading after the overview should still take it away.
- No primitives are introduced in detail (manifold, fields, deformations, etc.). Each is a sub-page; the overview only catalogues them.
- The "provisional" note at the end is load-bearing — it sets the reader's expectations for the open-questions page and for the schema's WIP framing.
