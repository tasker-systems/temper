A · Constellation — evolve today's dark editorial serif

Near-black canvas. The word is the node (serif). Homes split by hue+fill: authored nodes glow warm & filled; context resources are cool open rings. Regions are faint luminous washes named at their centroid. Edges are thin light strokes; weight = luminosity+thickness.

B · Atlas  ✓ CHOSEN — cartographic, denser, legible-at-1000

Slate canvas, less atmosphere. Regions become tinted territories with hull outlines & map-style labels. Nodes are compact chips (dot + label) — homes split by fill vs. outline, doc-type by dot hue. Denser and more scannable; an explicit edge legend. Leans utilitarian over moody.

Both encode the same substrate truthfully: home = fill vs. outline, doc-type = hue (concept amber · question violet · memory green), edge kind = line style, polarity = arrow, weight = thickness, region = hull/wash, history = the selected node's trail in the rail. The choice is mostly mood + density.
