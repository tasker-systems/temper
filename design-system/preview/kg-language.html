KNOWLEDGE GRAPH · VISUAL LANGUAGE
How the typographic pattern from the diagrams becomes an interactive graph. Participants are words, aggregators are frames, edges are hairlines, color pours from the source.

PARTICIPANTS · the word is the node

AGGREGATORS · the frame is the container · children live inside

Frame colour echoes the aggregator type at low alpha. Children are typeset inside — they participate in the outer graph too.

EDGES · stroke encodes type · color pours from source on hover

DEPENDS_ON solid · structural certainty
EXTENDS solid · builds upon
PRECEDED_BY long-dash · temporal sequence
RELATES_TO medium-dash · soft association
REFERENCES dotted · citation, weakest
EMERGENT hairline arc · Jaccard-derived · meta-doc mode only

HOVER · color pours from the source along its relationships

Hover over any node. Its connected edges brighten and take on the source's colour at the source end, fading to parchment at the target. Untouched edges drop to rgba(255,255,255,0.06) — the rest of the graph quietens.

MODE TOGGLE · typography, not chrome

VIEW
structural meta-doc
Active mode is serif, in full parchment. Inactive is mono-caps, dimmed. Click the inactive word to swap — graph re-renders with a 220ms cross-fade.

ZOOM TIERS · typography emerges with proximity

OVERVIEW · ≤ 25%
Frames and dots. Structure only.
NEIGHBORHOOD · 25–75%
Words near viewport center. Periphery stays dots.
DETAIL · > 75%
Full typography. Stroke patterns. Session halos as marginalia.

SESSIONS · annotation layer, not graph participants

Sessions don't clutter the graph. Each resource gets a small mono-cap count in session green to its upper-right — ⌊3⌋ means "three sessions reference this." Click the halo to reveal the session list as a side panel.
